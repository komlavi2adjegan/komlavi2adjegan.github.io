📑 Ongoing Research

Dissertation: Improved Water Infrastructure and Households' Living Conditions in Sub-Saharan Africa: Lessons from Peri-Urban Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC). Advisors: Flore Gubert & Camille Saint-Macary. Pre-defense: July 17, 2026 · Final defense expected: November 2026. Three research projects in water economics and community-led management, drawn from the longitudinal Eau, Assainissement et Conditions de Vie des Ménages (EACV) survey conducted in ten peri-urban districts of Kinshasa, DRC (2018–2021).

  1. Job Market Paper
    Community-Led Water Projects, Social Capital, and Water Service Sustainability: Mixed-Methods Evidence from Peri-Urban Kinshasa, DRC

    Evaluates the PILAEP program and local water users' associations (ASUREPs) using panel data combined with qualitative fieldwork. Key finding: declining social capital does not necessarily undermine service provision.

    DIAL researcher profile
  2. Modeling Household Water Source Choice in a Water-Rich Context: Evidence from Peri-Urban Kinshasa, DRC
    with Virginie Comblon, Flore Gubert, Camille Saint-Macary

    Discrete-choice models applied to geo-referenced data on all water points, combined with detailed household-level data on domestic water use.

  3. Does Better Access to Safe Water Foster Human Capital? A Quasi-Experimental Study from Peri-Urban Kinshasa, DRC
    with Virginie Comblon, Flore Gubert, Camille Saint-Macary · quasi-experimental design

    Finds that improvements in water access did not produce significant improvements in children's health or educational outcomes over the study period.
