About

I am a development economist specializing in applied microeconomics, econometrics, and impact evaluation. My research interests lie in the economics and political economy of education, health, basic service delivery, community-driven development, water infrastructure, labor market participation, and social capital in Sub-Saharan Africa. I have extensive experience designing and implementing large-scale surveys, conducting stakeholder interviews, and applying mixed-methods research that integrates rigorous quantitative analysis with qualitative fieldwork to generate policy-relevant evidence.

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at Université Paris Dauphine-PSL, affiliated with LEDa-DIAL, and a visiting PhD research fellow at ENSEA (Abidjan, Côte d'Ivoire). My dissertation, Improved Water Infrastructure and Households' Living Conditions in Sub-Saharan Africa: Lessons from Peri-Urban Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of Congo, is advised by Flore Gubert and Camille Saint-Macary. I hold a Master's in Development Economics from Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne (IEDES) and a degree in Agricultural Engineering from the Université de Lomé (Togo).
